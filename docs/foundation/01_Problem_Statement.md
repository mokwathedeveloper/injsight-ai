# InjSight AI Problem Statement

## Product Name

**InjSight AI**

## Product Positioning

**AI Wallet Intelligence for Injective DeFi**

InjSight AI turns Injective wallet, portfolio, and on-chain activity data into clear AI-generated insights, risk scores, alerts, and reports for traders and DeFi teams.

---

## Problem Statement

DeFi users, traders, and protocol teams operating within the Injective ecosystem have access to large amounts of public on-chain data, but that data is often difficult to interpret, fragmented across tools, and presented in formats that require technical or financial expertise to understand.

A wallet may contain token balances, portfolio exposure, transaction history, DeFi interactions, trading activity, and changing risk patterns. However, most existing blockchain explorers and dashboards show this information as raw data: wallet addresses, token denominations, transaction hashes, timestamps, contract interactions, and balance tables. While this information is technically available, it is not easily understandable or actionable for many users.

For individual traders and DeFi users, this creates a major usability gap. They may be able to see what assets they hold, but they often struggle to understand what those holdings mean in practical terms. They may not know whether their portfolio is overly concentrated, whether their exposure has changed significantly, whether recent activity has increased risk, or what parts of their wallet deserve closer attention.

For DeFi teams, DAOs, and protocols, the problem is even larger. Teams need to monitor wallet behavior, treasury exposure, whale activity, liquidity movement, and user engagement patterns. However, doing this manually requires checking explorers, spreadsheets, dashboards, and internal tools. This slows decision-making and makes it difficult to quickly identify meaningful changes in on-chain behavior.

The core problem is not lack of data. The problem is that on-chain data is difficult to understand, difficult to summarize, and difficult to convert into clear intelligence.

## Expanded Problem Statement

The Injective ecosystem is designed for high-performance DeFi activity, including trading, token transfers, financial applications, and on-chain interactions. As this activity grows, users and teams need better ways to understand what is happening across wallets and portfolios.

However, the current experience is still highly technical. A user who enters a wallet address into an explorer may see balances and transactions, but they are left to interpret the meaning themselves. This creates several problems.

### 1. Raw Data Is Not the Same as Insight

Most blockchain tools display information, but they do not explain it.

A user can see:

```txt
Wallet holds INJ, USDT, and other assets.
```

But they may not understand:

```txt
This wallet is highly exposed to INJ price movement, has limited stablecoin balance, and may be more vulnerable to volatility than a more diversified portfolio.
```

The gap between data and interpretation is where users lose value.

### 2. Portfolio Risk Is Hard to Understand

Many users hold assets without fully understanding their exposure. They may not know whether their wallet is concentrated in one token, whether they have enough stablecoin balance, or whether recent transactions have changed their risk profile.

This is especially important in DeFi, where market conditions can change quickly and wallet activity can become difficult to track manually.

### 3. On-Chain Activity Is Fragmented

Wallet activity can include transfers, swaps, staking, trading, deposits, withdrawals, and interactions with DeFi protocols. These actions may appear across multiple tools or raw transaction views.

Users and teams often have to move between explorers, dashboards, and spreadsheets to understand what happened.

This creates friction and slows decision-making.

### 4. Non-Technical Users Are Excluded

Many users are interested in DeFi but are not comfortable reading transaction hashes, smart contract interactions, denominations, or raw wallet data.

This makes DeFi less accessible.

InjSight AI helps make wallet intelligence easier to understand by translating complex on-chain information into plain English.

### 5. Protocol Teams Need Better Wallet Intelligence

DeFi teams and DAOs need to understand wallet behavior at a higher level. They may want to monitor treasury wallets, whale movements, liquidity changes, and user behavior trends.

Without AI-powered summaries and alerts, teams are forced to rely on manual monitoring or complex analytics workflows.

This limits speed, visibility, and operational efficiency.
